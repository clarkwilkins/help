Cash position analysis helps you understand, in both a current and historical timeframe, your company's cash and assets positions.

Chart 1 is cash position by account. It's filtered to only show cash accounts.

Chart 2 is asset position by account. This includes all current and fixed asset accounts you may be using in your chart of accounts.

Chart 3 tracks the company cash-on-hand over time. Each date is a total of the cash you held at the end of the day.

Chart 4 uses the same data as chart 3 but is charting the change per day. If you have a net flow of cash out of the company, the chart will dip below zero.

Below the charts is a summary of the data in a table format:

date
Only dates where something happens in a cash account are included.
account name
The account number and name are shown here.1
change
The amount that flowed in or out of the account.
total
The total balance of cash after accounting for this change.

NOTES
  1. For further investigation, you would want to use the registers in the Chart of Accounts.
