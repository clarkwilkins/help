There is no functional difference1 between receiving a customer payment and reapplying a customer credit against an invoice balance — other than the source of the “funding”. In the case of reapplication, the source is a customer credit which moved funds from a sales income account to the Adjustments and Refunds account.2

Begin by going to ☰ > tools, and then select credit memos. Your eligible credit memos will appear in open credit balances.3

  1. Click on +apply credits to get to the appropriate view.
  2. Normally, you would leave the entity selector alone here.4 If you need to switch directly, you can do so now.
  3. Select a credit memo with an open balance.
  4. All open balances for the client are now shown. Apply credits as desired, but make sure remaining amount available does not go below zero.5
  5. Click apply credits to save your changes.

NOTES
  1. In the latter case (discussed here), we use a different transaction type and pointers internally, and the accounting is all done in the 3xx accounts (Income).
  2. So, the net effect, when all credits have been refunded or applied to other balances, is the balance in Adjustments and Refunds goes to zero.
  3. If this section is not present, you have nothing eligible for a refund, and a message to that effect should be present.
  4. If you are not running more than one set of company accounts, this selector will not appear.
  5. This means you are applying more credit than is available.
