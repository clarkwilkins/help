This analysis is based on the aggregate spend per category per month. This is accrual-basis analysis — no distinction is made on whether an expense has been paid or not. There are two views which consider this data from different perspectives.

expense breakdown

The view is a series of pie-charts, one per month, for the selected year. The slices are sorted by spend amount and displayed as a total with categories with the highest spend in the month shown first.1

For further detail, click on any chart to toggle a complete breakdown of the data with subtotals and percentage of total expense in the month.

You can go even further by clicking on a row in the results table to switch to advanced expense search and see all of the expenses in the subtotal.

cost trends

The view shows all expenses that reach a threshold of at least 2.5% during any one month period in the year. The values shown are scaled as a percentage of the entire expenses in the month. For example, if you have $5000.00 in total costs in January and the rent is $2000.00, the rent line will be at 40% for that month.

diving deeper

These are broad trend visualization tools for looking at the composition of expenses. If you need visualization of a specific category like Rent, move to the expense visualization tool.2 For specific data on what happened, run a targeted expense search.2

NOTES
  1. Expense categories that account for < 2.5% of the monthly total are combined into an “Other” category. The actual percentage is a bit erratic at the time this article was written — seems to be hit or miss on displaying a value due to something in Google Charts.
  2. Both of these tools are available in the analysis options.
