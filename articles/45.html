Normally, items are constructed from existing items in inventory. To find an item, you use “search terms”. The search triggers automatically and returns any items that are a match on part name, part number/sku, or item number. Any matches found are grouped by part number and displayed along with information on how many are available and what condition they are in.

part numbers
Any item where your search string appears within the part number is included.
part names
Any item where your search string appears within the part name is included.
item number
If an item's unique number matches your input, it's included.

If you use “search terms” and find a match, you can click on it. This causes two actions: the item description is automatically loaded with the part name and number from the clicked selection, and sku trend analysis is performed.

Whether you trigger from “search terms” or not, to add an item, you must enter the item description. Optionally, you can also add rate 1 and rate 2. When rate 1 is less than rate 2, this is considered an exchange sale. If rate 1 is set equal or higher than rate 2, it's considered an outright sale.

Inventory analysis

In the item description, you will also see analysis of your current stock composition. The first line shows items available to quote — divided into good and bad item counts (set by the item condition). The second line shows where these items are located, so you can make additional decisions based on factors such as availability and whether you trust the item(s) to be available.
