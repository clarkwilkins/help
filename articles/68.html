Followups are an organizational/quasi-reminder system that you use to set a date when you need to look at something — to follow up on it. For example, stockd automatically creates a followup for the next day whenever you quote a client. Another example is sending an item to repair, and setting a followup to check on it 14 days later.

Followups can be set on:

  • Sales quotes
  • Sales orders
  • Returns records
  • Repair jobs
  • Inventory items
  • Tasks

There are two components: the followup date, and an optional assignment linking responsibility to a specific user.1

Followups are always set from a date control labelled followup on the item.

Followups appear on the ☰ menu when they come due, and they turn red from the next day until they are cleared. Clearing is done from the list (-clear) or from the item — clearing the followup date by clicking on it and accessing the date handler_.

NOTES
  1. You may consider adding a note if you are assigning the followup to another user.
