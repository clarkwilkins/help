The term “special accounts” refers to these three accounts which are built-for-purpose.

adjustments and refunds1
When a credit memo posts, it creates a transaction transferring the memo amount from a sales income account to this account — pending a refund or reapplication of the funds against another balance due. While funds remain in this account, the balance sheet total for income remains unaffected.
undeposited funds2
This is a special cash account that holds undeposited payments.
employer payroll tax3
When an employee's payroll is processed, the employer typically pays a matching tax contribution for FICA and Medicare expenses (and possibly other taxes). These costs are tax-deductible expenses, and as such are to be grouped in a non-overhead expense account.

You can run accountd without these settings. However, certain reporting functions, such as credit memo analysis, won't be able to function unless these accounts are designated.

NOTES
  1. Typically this account is called 399.000: Adjustments and Refunds.
  2. Typically this account is called 105.000: Undeposited Funds.
  3. Typically this account is called 586.000: Payroll Taxes.
